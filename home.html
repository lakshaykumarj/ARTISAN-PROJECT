Who are Artisans?
Artisans play an important role in preserving traditional craftsmanship and cultural heritage.They use sustainable materials and methods to create their products, which can have a positive impact on the environment.Many artisans operate small businesses that contribute to local economies and support the livelihoods of their communities.They are skilled craftsmen who create unique and often handmade products using traditional methods. Artisans continue to play an important role in preserving traditional craftsmanship and cultural heritage, and their work is valued.

Artisan Awareness
The artisan movement emerged in the late 19th and early 20th centuries as a response to the industrialization and mass production that had become the norm in Western societies. Artisans, who had previously worked independently or in small workshops, were being displaced by factories and machines that could produce goods faster and cheaper than human labor.
Artisans began to organize themselves into groups and societies that aimed to promote traditional craftsmanship and preserve the skills and techniques of their trade.
